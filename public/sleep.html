In today's society, it is not uncommon to stay awake for a prolonged time. However, the quality of our sleep impacts directly our condition and neglecting it may prove hazardous for your health.

A mysterious process

The process behind sleep is still not fully understand. However, it surely impacts the performances of the body. Studies have shown that a lack of sleep affects the attention, the mood, and the cognitive performances of the individual. Furthermore, people who lacks sleep tends to not notice it despite having real effects on their evryday life.

Strangely, everyone will not suffer from sleep loss the same way as others. For example, young adults are more vulnerable than older adults. There is also cases of sleep disorders which are dangerous, specially with patients affected by serious illnesses.

How to improve your sleep

Overall, it is recommended to have 8 hours of sleep everyday. However, the best advice is to have a good routine. This can be achieved by keeping a regular schedule, setting up a restful environnement and adapting your activities before going to bed.

There is obviously activities to avoid. You want your body to be relaxed, so reading a book can help preparing sleep time. On the contrary, avoid screens as the blue light may prevent you from falling asleep. Besides, any meal taken before bed should be light and must not contain caffein.

To conclude, your sleep time should be consistent and anticipated. However, if you don't feel sleepy, do not force it. Just take your time to relax and when your body will feel sleepy, you may go to bed.
